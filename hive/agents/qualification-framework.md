## 1. Lead Scoring Model

### Scoring Philosophy
Leads are scored on two axes:
- **Demographic (Static):** Company and contact fit with your ICP
- **Behavioral (Dynamic):** What they've done that signals intent

A combined score of **70+ = MQL / HQL → Pass to AE**

## 2. Qualification Questions — Discovery Framework

Use **MEDDIC** adapted for outbound. For cold outbound, lead with pain and urgency.

### MEDDIC Outbound Adaptation

**M — Metrics** *(Get specific numbers)*
> "What's your current average deal size / revenue run rate?"
> "How many reps on your team today?"
> "What's your target for next quarter?"

**E — Economic Buyer** *(Identify who controls the budget)*
> "Who else would be involved in a decision like this?"
> "What does your budget process look like for tools like this?"
> "Have you bought something similar before, and how did that work?"

**D — Decision Criteria** *(What are they judging solutions on?)*
> "What would a solution need to do for you to consider it a success?"
> "Is there a specific feature or outcome that's table stakes?"

**D — Decision Process** *(How do they actually buy?)*
> "Walk me through how you evaluate and sign off on new tools."
> "How long does something like this typically take from conversation to contract?"

**I — Identify Pain** *(Quantify the problem)*
> "What happens if you don't solve this? What's the cost of inaction?"
> "How much time / money / stress is this problem costing you right now?"

**C — Champion** *(Do they have internal influence?)*
> "How would you pitch this to your boss?"
> "Who else on your team would be excited about this?"

### Budget Questions (Ask Early)
- "What budget range are you working within for this?"
- "Have you already allocated spend for this quarter / fiscal year?"
- "If we can demonstrate ROI, how quickly could we move?"

### Timeline Questions (Ask Directly)
- "If we find a fit, when would you want to be up and running?"
- "What's driving this timing now vs. 6 months ago?"
- "Are there any hard deadlines we should know about?"

### Red Flag Phrases to Probe Further
- "We looked at this before" → Why didn't it stick? Who was involved?
- "We have something in place" → How satisfied are you? When does it renew?
- "We'll revisit this next year" → Push for specificity. "What would have to change?"

## 3. Red Flags / Disqualifiers

### Hard Disqualifiers (Do Not Pass to AE)
- ❌ No budget or budget not allocated
- ❌ No economic buyer access
- ❌ No clear timeline (within 6 months)
- ❌ Company in financial distress, layoffs, or acquisition
- ❌ No identified pain — "everything's fine"
- ❌ Political blockers that cannot be overcome (e.g., "our legal team would never approve this")
- ❌ Competitor already owns the relationship and has a contract
- ❌ Full-time SDR but trying to sell to another SDR team

### Soft Red Flags (Proceed with Caution / Demote Grade)
- ⚠️ "Not the right time" with no defined timeline → Probe harder
- ⚠️ Multiple stakeholders with competing priorities
- ⚠️ "We'll need IT approval" (IT blockers can kill deals)
- ⚠️ Company just went through org change / layoffs
- ⚠️ Contact is a "champion" but has no budget authority
- ⚠️ Long sales cycle stated (12+ months) but no clear reason why
- ⚠️ Contact is only engaging with you, not other stakeholders

### Disqualification Scripts
- **Budget objection:** "Totally understand — I want to make sure we're not wasting your time. Is there a budget range you can share, or is it more that this isn't a priority right now?"
- **Timing objection:** "I hear you. Just so I can follow up at the right time — what would need to change for this to become a priority? A new hire? A budget cycle? A company event?"
- **Authority objection:** "Makes sense — who would typically be involved in a decision like this? Would it be worth looping them in now so we can all be aligned?"

## 4. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Definition

### ICP Template

| Dimension | Your ICP |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | [e.g., SaaS, FinTech, Healthcare Tech] |
| **Company Stage** | [e.g., Series A-C, $5M-$50M ARR] |
| **Company Size** | [e.g., 50-500 employees] |
| **Geography** | [e.g., US/Canada, English-speaking EMEA] |
| **Tech Stack Signals** | [e.g., Uses Salesforce, Segment, HubSpot] |
| **Hiring Signals** | [e.g., Growing sales team, hiring AEs] |
| **Funding Signals** | [e.g., Raised Series B in last 18 months] |
| **Department** | [e.g., Sales, RevOps, Marketing, CS] |
| **Buyer Title** | [e.g., VP of Sales, Head of Growth, CRO] |
| **Pain Points** | [e.g., Low quota attainment, long ramp time, poor CRM data quality] |
| **Alternatives Considered** | [e.g., Currently using spreadsheets, no formal tool] |
| **Buying Trigger Events** | [e.g., New VP hired, missed Q4 targets, new funding round] |

### Look-Alike Signals
- Similar tech stack to existing customers (Clearbit, Apollo enrichment)
- Same industry and stage
- Same hiring patterns (e.g., 10+ new SDRs hired in last 6 months)
- Same geography
- Similar company structure (e.g., "has an SDR team" signals readiness)

## 5. Qualification Process Flow

### Stage 1: Prospecting / Initial Research (SDR)
- [ ] Verify ICP fit (company size, industry, geography)
- [ ] Enrich contact data (title, phone, email, LinkedIn)
- [ ] Score lead: A/B/C/D
- [ ] Identify research hooks (recent news, LinkedIn activity, tech stack)

### Stage 2: First Outreach (SDR)
- [ ] Send Day 0 email with personalization
- [ ] Cold call Day 1-2 if phone number available
- [ ] LinkedIn connection Day 1

### Stage 3: Sequence / Engagement (SDR)
- [ ] Execute 7-touch multi-channel sequence
- [ ] Track replies — any reply triggers response protocol
- [ ] Score behavioral signals (opens, clicks, replies)
- [ ] Re-score lead after engagement

### Stage 4: Discovery Call (SDR or AE)
- [ ] Book 20-30 min call
- [ ] Run MEDDIC discovery questions
- [ ] Score on qualification criteria
- [ ] Confirm ICP fit verbally
- [ ] Identify next steps + timeline

### Stage 5: AE Handoff (SDR → AE)
- [ ] Complete handoff notes in CRM:
  - Discovery summary (pain, budget, timeline, authority)
  - Key quotes / objections
  - Next steps agreed upon
  - ICP fit rationale
- [ ] Schedule intro call between SDR, AE, and prospect
- [ ] SDR stays on for first meeting as support

### Stage 6: Opportunity / Disqualified
- **Qualified:** Pass to AE as Opportunity, set next steps
- **Disqualified:** Remove from sequence, log reason, add to appropriate nurture or suppression list

## 6. Output Grades — Final Classification

### Grade A — Hot Lead
- ✅ Matches ICP on all 3+ dimensions
- ✅ Budget authority or direct access to budget holder
- ✅ Clear pain point identified
- ✅ Timeline: ready to buy within 30-90 days
- ✅ Champion identified and engaged
- **Action:** Immediate AE handoff. Priority meeting booking. Don't let this go cold.

### Grade B — Warm Lead
- ✅ Matches ICP on 2+ dimensions
- ⚠️ Some qualification gaps (timeline unclear, budget not confirmed, authority uncertain)
- ✅ Engaged with 3+ touches
- **Action:** Continue outreach. Book a discovery call. Surface to AE with "conditional" handoff.

### Grade C — Nurture Lead
- ⚠️ Partial ICP fit OR weak behavioral signals
- ❌ No reply yet OR single reply with low intent
- **Action:** Move to long-term nurture sequence. Re-engage in 60-90 days with new angle.

### Grade D — Suppress
- ❌ No ICP fit
- ❌ Hard disqualifier (see Red Flags section)
- ❌ Unreachable after 10+ touches
- **Action:** Remove from active sequences. Add to suppression list. Do not re-engage without a trigger event.

## Quick Reference: Pre-Handoff Checklist

Before passing a lead to AE, confirm all 6:

- [ ] **Budget:** Do they have money allocated or authority to allocate it?
- [ ] **Authority:** Are they the decision-maker or can they get the decision-maker in the room?
- [ ] **Need:** Can they articulate a specific pain that your product solves?
- [ ] **Timeline:** Can they commit to a specific window (Q1, next fiscal, next 90 days)?
- [ ] **Champion:** Do they have internal credibility to push this forward?
- [ ] **Fit:** Does this company look like your best existing customers?

|---|---|---|
| 6/6 | A+ | Immediate AE handoff |
| 4-5/6 | B | Conditional handoff |
| 2-3/6 | C | Nurture |
| 0-1/6 | D | Suppress |
