## 1. Platform-by-Platform Playbook

### LinkedIn
- **Best for:** B2B, professional services, thought leadership, long-form articles
- **Format:** Carousels (highest organic reach), single images, short-form text posts, articles, documents
- **Algorithm favors:** Comments > Saves > Clicks > Reactions — optimize for saves and comments
- **Ideal posting time:** Tues–Thurs, 7–9am and 12–1pm local time (before/around work hours)
- **Key tactic:** End posts with a question to drive comments; carousels get 3-5x more reach than single-image posts
- **Don't:** Drop links in posts (algorithm suppresses them) — use them in comments instead

### Twitter/X
- **Best for:** Real-time commentary, news, opinions, quick tips, building personal brand
- **Format:** Text-first threads, single tweets, images, short videos (2:20 max)
- **Algorithm favors:** Early engagement velocity — replies and retweets in the first 30 minutes matter most
- **Ideal posting time:** 8–10am and 6–8pm local time; align with US timezone for broader reach
- **Key tactic:** Threads outperform single tweets; tweet 3–5x/day for best visibility
- **Don't:** Go silent for days — consistency triggers the algorithm

### Instagram
- **Best for:** Lifestyle, visual storytelling, personal brand (founders/CEOs), product aesthetics
- **Format:** Reels (highest reach), carousels (educational), Stories (daily touchpoints), Lives
- **Algorithm favors:** Reels above all; saves and shares over likes
- **Ideal posting time:** 9–11am and 6–9pm local time
- **Key tactic:** 3–5 Reels per week minimum; Stories daily for algorithm signal + retention
- **Don't:** Over-produce at the expense of frequency; authenticity beats polish

### YouTube
- **Best for:** Long-form educational content, tutorials, interviews, documentary-style storytelling
- **Format:** Long-form (8–20 min optimal), Shorts (under 60 sec), community posts
- **Algorithm favors:** Watch time and click-through rate on thumbnails/titles — both matter equally
- **Ideal posting time:** Consistent schedule beats perfect timing; publish when your audience is most active (check Analytics)
- **Key tactic:** Hook in first 30 seconds or lose viewers; end screens and cards drive watch time
- **Don't:** Upload without a thumbnail strategy or SEO title/description

### TikTok
- **Best for:** Reaching younger audiences (18–34), trending topics, rapid-fire education, behind-the-scenes
- **Format:** Short videos (15 sec–3 min), stitched videos, lives
- **Algorithm favors:** Watch time, engagement, and completion rate — make content that holds attention to the end
- **Ideal posting time:** 6–9pm daily; test with your niche audience
- **Key tactic:** Hook in the first 2 seconds; use trending sounds; educational content that feels entertaining works best
- **Don't:** Cross-post from Instagram Reels without editing — TikTok users can tell

### Newsletters (Substack, Beehiiv, Ghost)
- **Best for:** Building owned audience, deep relationships, paid subscriptions, direct revenue
- **Format:** Weekly or biweekly emails (consistency > frequency), pure text often outperforms designed templates
- **Algorithm favors:** N/A — it's owned media, but deliverability depends on open rates and spam complaints
- **Key tactic:** Send on a predictable schedule so readers expect you; the best newsletters feel like letters from a friend
- **Don't:** Get stuck in the sub-500-subscriber ceiling — focus on growth levers (referral programs, social cross-promotion) early

### Podcasts
- **Best for:** Authority building, long-form conversations, reaching commuters and multitaskers
- **Format:** Interview-based or solo monologue; 30–60 min is the sweet spot for most niches
- **Key tactic:** Appear as a guest on established podcasts before launching your own — guesting is a faster audience builder
- **Don't:** Launch without a distribution plan (YouTube, Spotify, Apple, YouTube clips) — audio-only limits reach

## 2. Content Adaptation: One Piece, Many Platforms

The core skill is **atomization** — taking one source piece and reshaping it for each platform's native format.

### The Content Atomization Workflow

```
Pillar Piece (long-form blog/video, 2,000+ words or 20+ min)
    ↓
Extract #1: Key insight → tweet thread (5–10 tweets)
    ↓
Extract #2: Best section → LinkedIn carousel (8–10 slides)
    ↓
Extract #3: Hook moment → TikTok/Reel (60–90 sec)
    ↓
Extract #4: Main argument → newsletter excerpt (300–500 words)
    ↓
Extract #5: Supporting stat → Instagram Story series
    ↓
Extract #6: Q&A from comments → YouTube Short
```

### Adaptation Rules by Platform

| Platform | Adaptation Rule |
|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | Make it professional even if the source is personal; "I learned this the hard way" becomes "Most teams make this mistake" |
| **Twitter/X** | Lead with the hottest take; save nuance for the thread; every tweet must stand alone |
| **Instagram** | Visual-first; text overlays must be readable on mobile; hook in first 3 seconds of Reels |
| **TikTok** | Conversational tone; raw beats polished; embrace the specific niche detail |
| **YouTube** | Expand for depth; add context and proof you couldn't fit elsewhere; thumbnail = headline |
| **Newsletter** | Longest-form adaptation; voice can be most personal; no character limits |

## 3. Repurposing Frameworks

### The 3-10-1 Rule
Create **3 pieces of long-form content per month**. From each long-form piece, extract **10 derivative pieces** (threads, carousels, snippets, clips). **Publish 1 piece per day**.

### Pillar + Cluster Model
- **1 Pillar page** (comprehensive guide, 3,000–5,000 words) targets one primary keyword
- **5–10 Cluster articles** (800–1,500 words) target related long-tail keywords and link back to the pillar
- This structure builds topical authority and captures search traffic across a topic area

### Content Repurposing Tiers

| Tier | Content Type | Purpose | Repurpose Into |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Long-form blog or YouTube video | SEO, thought leadership | Threads, carousels, newsletters, podcasts |
| **Tier 2** | Thread, newsletter, carousel | Engagement, shares | Clips, quotes, Story slides |
| **Tier 3** | Clips, quotes, Story posts | Awareness, retention | Ad creative, email sequences |

## 4. Distribution Channels: Owned, Earned, Paid

### Owned (Your Assets — Highest ROI Long-Term)
- **Website/blog** — SEO traffic, evergreen content
- **Email list** — highest-converting channel; treat it like gold
- **YouTube channel** — second-largest search engine; compounding returns over time
- **Podcasts** — builds deep audience relationships

### Earned (Third-Party Validation — Highest Trust)
- **Guest appearances** on podcasts, YouTube channels, newsletters
- **Press coverage** — HARO/QueryWatch responses, original data PR
- **Community shares** — someone else posting your content (give them easy share assets)
- **Referrals** — satisfied readers/shareholders who spread the word

### Paid (Fastest but Most Expensive)
- **LinkedIn Ads** — B2B, high CPC but high lead quality
- **Meta Ads** — broad reach, good for content engagement and newsletter growth
- **Sponsored emails** — newsletter sponsorships in adjacent audiences
- **Content amplification** — paying to promote top-performing organic posts

**Rule of thumb:** Earn organic reach before spending on paid. If content doesn't resonate without promotion, paid promotion won't fix it.

## 6. Measuring Success: Platform Metrics

### LinkedIn
- **Impressions** — vanity; ignore unless paired with engagement
- **Engagement rate** — (reactions + comments + saves) / impressions; benchmark: 2–5%
- **Comment quality** — are people adding substance or just "great post"?
- **Profile visits & search appearances** — signals of growing authority

### Twitter/X
- **Tweet impressions** — floor metric
- **Engagement rate** — benchmark: 1–3% is good; 5%+ is excellent
- **Follower growth rate** — track weekly; momentum > absolute number
- **Link clicks** — highest-intent action; if you're driving traffic, this matters most

### YouTube
- **Watch time (hours)** — the #1 algorithm signal
- **Click-through rate (CTR)** — thumbnail + title effectiveness; benchmark: 2–10%
- **Average view duration** — content quality signal; compare to video length
- **Subscriber conversion** — % of viewers who subscribe; 2–5% is strong

### Newsletter
- **Open rate** — benchmark: 35–50% for well-run B2B newsletters
- **Click rate** — benchmark: 2–5%; indicates content-to-offer alignment
- **Unsubscribe rate** — should be under 0.5%/send; spikes mean content mismatch

### TikTok/Reels
- **Video views (plays)** — first-pass metric
- **Watch time & completion rate** — 80%+ completion = strong content
- **Like-to-view ratio** — benchmark: 3–8%
- **Shares** — highest-value engagement; means the content resonated enough to share

## Distribution Strategy Summary

1. **Own your audience first** — email list and owned channels beat platform dependence
2. **One pillar piece → many derivative pieces** — atomize ruthlessly
3. **Match format to platform** — don't cross-post without adapting
4. **Consistency beats perfection** — publish on schedule even when content isn't "perfect"
5. **Engage as much as you publish** — distribution isn't just posting; it's participating
6. **Measure what matters** — engagement quality > vanity metrics
7. **Build relationships, not just content** — distribution is ultimately about people trusting you enough to share your work
