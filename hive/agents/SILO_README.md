# Agent Private Silo — Template

Each agent has their own private silo. This is their personal memory space — nobody else reads it unless the agent asks for help.

## Files

### `log.md`
Personal working notes. The agent's running journal of:
- What you're currently working on
- Ideas and observations
- Decisions made and why
- What you learned that doesn't need to be in the shared hive

### `context.md`
Your agent-specific state:
- How you prefer to work
- What you're currently focused on
- Relationships with other agents (e.g., "SDR Beta: coordinates with SDR Alpha")
- Anything that helps you get back up to speed after a gap

### `memory.md`
Private learnings — things you learned that:
- Don't need to be in the shared hive
- Are specific to your way of working
- You want to remember but don't need others to know

## Silo Rules

- Your silo is YOURS. Nobody else reads it.
- The curator doesn't read your silo unless you explicitly ask for help.
- Keep it organized enough to be useful when you return after a gap.
- You can delete or reorganize your silo however you like.
- Your silo is never auto-cleaned or archived by the curator.

## Privacy

Private silos are not part of the shared hive. They don't sync to Git, don't feed into the curator's review, and don't appear in any shared documentation. They exist solely to give you continuity between sessions.
