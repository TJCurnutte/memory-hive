## 1. The SDR-to-AE Handoff: Why It Matters

The SDR-to-AE handoff is one of the highest-leverage moments in the sales cycle. Done well, it accelerates deals, builds trust, and prevents the dreaded "start over from scratch" moment. Done poorly, it kills deals, wastes the SDR's work, and creates a poor prospect experience.

### What Gets Lost in Bad Handoffs
- Context: The AE doesn't know the prospect's history.
- Momentum: The prospect has to repeat themselves.
- Trust: The prospect feels like they're talking to strangers.
- Deal speed: Time is lost re-qualifying.

## 2. Pre-Handoff Prep

### Before Any Handoff, Confirm:
- [ ] Prospect is actually qualified (budget, authority, need, timeline).
- [ ] You have a documented demo/meeting completed.
- [ ] Decision-maker is identified (or will be in the meeting).
- [ ] Next steps are agreed upon.
- [ ] Prospect has explicitly said they're willing to move forward.
- [ ] You have at least 3 touchpoints logged in CRM.

### The Golden Handoff Criteria
1. **Qualified opportunity**: Firmographic + demographic fit confirmed.
2. **Meeting booked**: Real calendar booking, not just interest.
3. **Champion identified**: Someone internally who wants this to happen.
4. **Clear pain documented**: The specific problem your solution solves.
5. **Budget range confirmed**: At least a ballpark.
6. **Timeline established**: When they need a solution.
7. **Next step set**: What happens after the AE meeting.

BUDGET:

TIMELINE:
- Want to solve by: [Date]

- [Objections raised and responses given]

- [Anything that will help the AE — personal context, communication style, etc.]
- [Best time to reach / preferred contact method]
- [Language used by champion to describe the problem]

\`\`\`

### What Belongs in the Handoff Doc
- **Context**: Everything the AE needs to pick up without re-explaining.
- **Pain**: The specific business problem, quantified if possible.
- **History**: Key interactions, calls, emails, demos.
- **Champion sentiment**: How motivated is the internal advocate?
- **Budget + Timeline**: Confirmed or best estimate.
- **Red flags**: Any concerns or objections already surfaced.
- **Talking points**: What resonated in prior calls.

### What Does NOT Belong in the Handoff Doc
- Generic marketing talking points.
- Speculation without evidence.
- Information you don't actually have.
- Complaints about the prospect.
- Pressure tactics to force the AE to prioritize.

## 4. The Handoff Meeting

### Best Practices
- **Synchronous is best**: Live warm handoff call (SDR + AE + prospect).
- **Format**: SDR introduces AE, prospect reaffirms need, AE takes the baton.
- **Duration**: 10-15 minutes max.
- **Never blind handoff**: Prospect should always know AE is being assigned.

### Warm Handoff Script
> "Hi [Prospect], this is [SDR Name] from [Company]. I wanted to introduce you to [AE Name], who will be leading your evaluation from here. [AE Name] is one of our [X] experts focused on [their problem area], and I personally wanted to make sure you had the best support possible as you move forward. [AE Name], I'll let you take it from here — [Prospect] has been exploring [problem] for [company type], and they've specifically been looking at [key feature]. I'll stay on the call to answer any context questions but the conversation is yours."

### Post-Handoff Email
After the warm handoff call, send a follow-up email:
- Thank the prospect.
- Confirm next steps and timeline.
- Attach the handoff brief (optional — confirm with prospect).
- Set a specific follow-up date.

## 5. Setting Expectations with Prospects

### Key Conversations to Have Early

#### Expectation: Timeline
> "Most of our customers in [industry] typically move through evaluation in [X-Y weeks]. Here's what the process looks like: [Step 1] → [Step 2] → [Step 3]. We can move faster if [condition]."

#### Expectation: Involvement
> "This evaluation will likely involve [titles] from your side — typically [job titles]. What does that process look like at [Company]?"

#### Expectation: Pricing
> "Our pricing is [model — per seat / flat fee / custom]. Most companies like yours land in the $[X-Y]/month range. We'll get into specifics in the [next step]."

#### Expectation: Technical Evaluation
> "For technical validation, we typically do [X]. Does that work with your team's bandwidth?"

#### Expectation: Decision Criteria
> "When you evaluate vendors, what are the top 3 things you're comparing? That'll help us make sure we're focusing on what matters most to you."

## 6. Trial-to-Paid Conversion

### Trial Design Best Practices
- **Set clear success criteria at trial start**: What does "success" look like?
- **Define the trial scope**: Limited features? Limited users? Limited time?
- **Schedule regular check-ins**: Don't wait until day 27 to check in.
- **Assign a trial champion**: One person responsible for driving adoption.
- **Create urgency**: Trial expires on [date] — what needs to happen before then?

### Trial-to-Paid Milestones
| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Welcome email + onboarding + success criteria set |
| Day 3 | Check-in: Getting started questions, blockers? |
| Day 7 | Progress review: What has been accomplished? |
| Day 14 | Mid-trial: Where are they on success criteria? |
| Day 21 | Trial close prep: What needs to happen next? |
| Day 27 | Final check: Decision timeline confirmation |
| Day 30 | Renewal conversation or exit interview |

### Trial Conversion Tactics
- **Usage-based triggers**: Alert CSM if trial user hits key activation events.
- **ROI review call**: Book a call to review their trial results.
- **Executive involvement**: Get champion's manager aware.
- **Reference customers**: Connect trial users with similar past customers.
- **Staged offer**: "If you convert by [date], we'll [incentive]."

## 7. Churn Prevention at Early Stage

### Warning Signs of Early Churn
- Low engagement after onboarding (no logins, no feature usage).
- Champion leaves the company or changes roles.
- No response to check-in emails/calls.
- "We're busy" — deferring meetings repeatedly.
- Competitor actively engaging your customer.
- Budget freeze or reorg announced.
- Single user account (no team adoption).

### Retention Plays for SDR/CS Handoff
1. **Documented success criteria**: If you know what they want to achieve, you can prove ROI.
2. **Executive sponsor engaged**: Even an email from a champion's manager helps.
3. **Multi-user adoption**: Don't let it be a single-user tool.
4. **Regular touchpoints**: Weekly at first, then bi-weekly, then monthly.
5. **Health score monitoring**: Track login frequency, feature adoption, NPS.

### CSM Handoff Checklist
- [ ] Introduce CSM via email from SDR with context.
- [ ] Share handoff brief with CSM.
- [ ] Schedule a joint call (SDR + CSM + prospect) for context.
- [ ] Confirm onboarding timeline and next steps.
- [ ] Set 30/60/90 day success milestones.
- [ ] Log all handoff details in CRM.

## 8. SDR Accountability in the Handoff

### What SDRs Are Responsible For
- Qualifying leads to standard before handoff.
- Documenting all prospect interactions.
- Building champion relationship.
- Creating and sending the handoff brief.
- Scheduling or initiating the warm handoff meeting.
- Not dumping cold leads on the AE.

### What AEs Are Responsible For
- Reviewing handoff brief before first call.
- Respecting the SDR's relationship.
- Taking the baton confidently.
- Closing the loop on outcomes (win/loss feedback).
- Not re-qualifying what's already qualified (without reason).

### Feedback Loop
SDRs should ask AEs monthly:
- What deals converted? What was the quality of the handoff?
- What deals stalled? Was it handoff quality or something else?
- What would have made this deal easier?
- Any intel from closed-lost deals?

## 9. Advanced Handoff Scenarios

### Multi-Stakeholder Handoffs
Enterprise deals often have 3-6 stakeholders. When multiple AEs or CSMs are involved:
- **Primary AE**: Owns the main deal relationship.
- **Technical AE/SE**: Handles technical evaluation.
- **CSM**: Manages post-sale relationship.
- **SDR's role**: Document each stakeholder's specific interest and involvement.

Handoff brief should include a stakeholder map:
```
STAKEHOLDER MAP
├── Economic Buyer: [Name, Title, Role]
│   └── Interest: Budget holder, final sign-off
├── Champion: [Name, Title, Role]
│   └── Interest: Driving the project internally
├── Technical Evaluator: [Name, Title, Role]
│   └── Interest: Technical fit, security, integration
├── Legal/Procurement: [Name, Title, Role]
│   └── Interest: Contract terms, compliance
└── End Users: [Names/Titles]
    └── Interest: Day-to-day usability, training needs
```

### Cross-Regional Handoffs
When a deal moves from one region to another:
- **Document time zone preferences**: Note best times for contact.
- **Language requirements**: Does the new AE need to speak the local language?
- **Cultural notes**: Include communication style preferences.
- **Legal/contracting differences**: Who signs in which entity?
- **Currency and pricing**: Confirm pricing is in local currency.

### SDR-to-CSM Handoff (Trial/Customer)
When SDR hands off a trial or activated customer to CSM:
1. **Send intro email from SDR**: "Meet your success partner, [CSM Name]."
2. **Share handoff brief with CSM**: All context in one doc.
3. **30-day success plan**: Agreed milestones for first 30 days.
4. **Risk flag**: Surface any early churn signals to CSM.
5. **SDR stays involved**: SDR can earn spiff on renewal if they stay engaged.

### Handoff Failures: Root Causes and Fixes

| Failure Mode | Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| AE doesn't prep for first call | Handoff doc not reviewed | Enforce handoff doc review before call |
| Prospect feels "handed off" | Cold handoff, no warm intro | Always do warm handoff call |
| AE re-qualifies what's already done | No trust in SDR qualification | Build AE trust via shared feedback loop |
| Context gets lost | Not documented in CRM | Enforce real-time CRM logging |
| Stalled deal handed off | SDR held too long | Set handoff criteria and enforce |
| Wrong AE assigned | Poor routing logic | Review routing rules quarterly |

## 10. Trial Management Deep Dive

### Trial Activation Sequence
**Week 1: Activation**
- Day 0: Welcome email (personal from CSM, not automated).
- Day 1: Onboarding session scheduled.
- Day 3: First check-in — are they stuck anywhere?
- Day 5: First feature activated — walk them through it.

**Week 2: Engagement**
- Day 10: Usage review — what's working, what's not?
- Day 14: Mid-point check-in. Are they on track toward success criteria?
- Day 14: Introduce second feature if first is going well.

**Week 3: Value Realization**
- Day 21: Usage deep dive. What has been the ROI so far?
- Day 21: Identify power users within trial team.
- Day 24: Executive sponsor brought into the conversation (optional).

**Week 4: Decision**
- Day 27: Final usage review. Have they hit success criteria?
- Day 28: Commercial conversation — pricing, contract terms.
- Day 30: Decision call — conversion or exit interview.

### Trial Success Criteria Examples

For a SaaS product evaluating trial success:
- **Minimum**: 3 team members active in the product.
- **Good**: 5+ team members, 2 features used, weekly usage.
- **Excellent**: All invited team members active, daily usage, data imported, admin configured.

For a service or consulting engagement:
- **Minimum**: 1 deliverable completed, positive feedback.
- **Good**: Milestones hit, stakeholder alignment achieved.
- **Excellent**: Champion presenting results internally.

### Trial Extension Strategy
When a trial needs more time:
1. **Assess root cause**: Are they blocked on technical, organizational, or strategic issues?
2. **Set new success criteria**: What must happen in the extension period?
3. **Create urgency**: Trial extension is not infinite — set a hard end date.
4. **Incentivize conversion**: Offer a bonus for early conversion at end of extension.
5. **Escalate internally**: Get champion's manager involved if needed.

### Trial-to-Paid Pricing Considerations
- **Annual vs. monthly**: Annual always preferred; offer discount (typically 10-20%).
- **Seat scaling**: Offer a "starter tier" if they're not ready for full rollout.
- **Pilot structure**: Formalize a paid pilot (30-90 days) if trial-to-paid conversion is uncertain.
- **Success-based pricing**: Tie pricing to outcomes (advanced but powerful).

## 11. Churn Prevention: Deep Dive

### Early Warning System for Churn

Create a "churn risk score" based on:
- **Engagement score**: Logins/week, features used, users active.
- **Champion engagement**: Last contact date, meeting response rate.
- **Support tickets**: Volume and severity of support tickets.
- **NPS score**: If collected, track trend over time.
- **Contract milestone proximity**: Churn risk peaks before renewal dates.

Risk tiers:
- 🟢 **Green (low risk)**: High engagement, strong champion, NPS 7+.
- 🟡 **Yellow (medium risk)**: Declining engagement, champion less responsive.
- 🔴 **Red (high risk)**: Very low engagement, champion gone, NPS <6.

### Retention Plays by Risk Tier

**Green (Low Risk)**
- Maintain regular touchpoints.
- Identify expansion opportunities.
- Ask for referral or case study.
- Keep champion engaged with new feature news.

**Yellow (Medium Risk)**
- Increase touchpoint frequency.
- Schedule executive sponsor check-in.
- Identify what broke and fix it.
- Set a specific "get back on track" meeting.

**Red (High Risk)**
- Immediate escalation to CSM manager.
- Immediate executive sponsor outreach.
- Root cause analysis: Why are they unhappy?
- Discount or concessions may be appropriate.
- Start identifying replacement prospects just in case.

### The "Champion Left" Play
When a champion changes roles or leaves:
1. **Immediate outreach**: "We're so glad to have worked with [Name]. Who is taking over?"
2. **Identify successor**: Find the new champion quickly.
3. **Ramp new champion**: Re-do onboarding if they've never used the product.
4. **Executive re-engagement**: Get leadership aware of the situation.
5. **Re-qualify**: Don't assume the new person shares the same vision.
