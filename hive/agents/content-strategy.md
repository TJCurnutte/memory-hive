## Content Pillars for AI Agents Companies

These are the core content categories that should drive your editorial calendar:

### Pillar 1: Education & How-To
- Tutorials on using AI agents
- Step-by-step guides
- "How to do X with AI agents" content
- Comparisons with manual processes
- **Why it works**: High save/share rate, positions you as the expert, attracts users actively looking for solutions

### Pillar 2: Product Demos & Showcases
- Short demos of features
- Before/after transformations
- Use case spotlights
- Customer success stories (with permission)
- **Why it works**: Converts interested observers, shows tangible value, drives trial signups

### Pillar 3: Thought Leadership & Industry Trends
- Take positions on where AI agents are headed
- Commentary on industry news
- Predictions and frameworks
- Analysis of market shifts
- **Why it works**: Builds brand authority, attracts press/journalists, generates discussion

### Pillar 4: Behind-the-Scenes & Culture
- Team spotlights
- Product development journeys
- Failures and lessons learned
- Office/remote culture content
- **Why it works**: Humanizes the brand, builds trust, differentiates from faceless competitors

### Pillar 5: Community & Engagement
- Polls asking for opinions
- "Reply with your use case" posts
- User-generated content reposts
- AMAs and Q&As
- **Why it works**: Builds loyalty, increases algorithm reach, generates social proof

## Content Types That Work for AI Agent Tools

### 1. **Tutorial Threads** ⭐
- 8–12 tweet threads walking through a specific use case
- Format: Hook → Context → Steps → Result → CTA
- Example: "I automated [X] in 10 minutes. Here's the exact prompt chain I used:"
- Works on: X, LinkedIn (as carousel)
- **Why it works**: Highly shareable, saves-worthy, demonstrates value directly

### 2. **Prompt Packs / Templates**
- Share useful prompts your audience can copy and use
- Format: Problem → Prompt → Variation tips
- Works on: X, LinkedIn, newsletters
- **Example hook**: "The prompt I use to [X] every single time:"
- **Tip**: Gate high-value ones behind email signup; share simpler ones freely

### 3. **Before/After Comparisons**
- Show the transformation: old way vs. AI agent way
- Format: Side-by-side text, short video, or carousel
- Works on: X, LinkedIn
- **Example**: "Before I used AI agents: [task] took 4 hours. After: 12 minutes."

### 4. **Use Case Spotlights**
- Spotlight a specific, concrete application of AI agents
- Format: Problem → Solution → Results (with numbers if possible)
- Works on: X, LinkedIn, blog
- **Tip**: Get user testimonials when possible (with permission)

### 5. **Industry News Commentary**
- React to AI/agent news within 24 hours
- Format: "Here's what this means for you" — context + opinion
- Works on: X, LinkedIn
- **Example**: "OpenAI just released [X]... here's why it matters for your AI agent stack"

### 6. **Tool Comparisons & Rankings**
- "X vs Y" or "Top 5 tools for [task]"
- Format: Carousel or long-form post
- Works on: X, LinkedIn, blog
- **Tip**: Include your tool fairly; don't oversell

### 7. **Mistakes & Lessons Learned**
- "The biggest mistake people make with AI agents" posts
- "What I wish I knew when I started" threads
- Works on: X, LinkedIn
- **Why it works**: Highly relatable, generates comments, positions you as experienced

### 8. **Roadmap & Product Teases**
- "What we're building next" posts
- Works on: X, LinkedIn, newsletters
- **Tip**: Tease, don't reveal everything; creates anticipation and engagement

## Hashtag Strategy

### On X/Twitter
- **Use 1–3 hashtags maximum**
- Research by searching your topic + seeing what top posts use
- High-performing AI agent hashtags:
  - #AIagents
  - #ArtificialIntelligence
  - #MachineLearning
  - #Automation
  - #NoCode
  - #AITools
  - #[SpecificToolNames] (when relevant)
- Mix: 1 broad (#AI) + 1–2 specific (#AIagents)
- Never use more than 3 — looks spammy and hurts reach

### On LinkedIn
- **Use 3–5 relevant hashtags**
- LinkedIn hashtags are essential for discoverability
- Strategy: 2–3 industry + 1–2 topic + 1 trend
- Example for AI agents post:
  - #ArtificialIntelligence
  - #Automation
  - #FutureOfWork
  - #MachineLearning
  - #Productivity

### Researching Hashtags
1. Search the hashtag on each platform — look at what content uses it
2. Check which hashtags top creators in your space use
3. Use tools like Scheduled or Circleboom for hashtag analytics
4. Track which hashtags drive the most engagement on your posts

### Rules
- Always include the hashtag at the end of the post OR in a reply
- Don't add irrelevant hashtags for reach — hurts credibility
- Case study: some hashtags get 10x more reach for specific content types

## Repurposing Strategy

**Core principle**: One piece of content → 8–10 posts

### Example: Blog Post / Long Thread
1. **Tweet thread** → breaks down the main points
2. **LinkedIn post** → single-post summary with carousel for steps
3. **Short video** → 60-second "key insight" clip
4. **Infographic** → visual summary for both platforms
5. **Email newsletter excerpt** → hook + link to full content
6. **Poll** → "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?"
7. **Quote graphic** → one compelling line from the piece
8. **Quote tweet** → react to it with your own 2-sentence take

### Example: Demo / Tutorial Video
1. **Full video** → native upload to X or LinkedIn
2. **Clip 1** → "Here's the mistake most people make" (hook)
3. **Clip 2** → "The setup I use" (feature showcase)
4. **Text post** → step-by-step recap
5. **Carousel** → visual walkthrough of steps
6. **Thread** → expanded version with extra tips
7. **Poll** → "Would you prefer [Option A] or [Option B]?"

### Cross-Platform Tweaks
- X: More casual, opinionated, uses slang
- LinkedIn: More professional framing, starts with personal story

## Engagement Strategy for AI Agents Community

### Building Community
1. **Reply to every comment** in first hour (boosts algorithm)
2. **Engage with similar accounts** — comment thoughtfully on their posts (not generic)
3. **Run monthly AMAs** — "Ask me anything about AI agents"
4. **Create a recurring series** — e.g., "AI Agent Tip Tuesday"
5. **Repost user content** with credit when people use your tool
6. **Respond to DMs** — especially from people who have questions

### Engagement Tactics That Work
- **Reply-gating**: Post a take and say "Hot or not? Reply below"
- **Community questions**: "Drop your biggest AI agent challenge in the comments"
- **Debates**: Take a position and invite disagreement — drives comments
- **Threads that invite replies**: End with "What's your experience with this?"

### Growth Levers
- Collaborate with adjacent creators (no-code tools, productivity, SaaS)
- Cross-post on both platforms consistently
- Engage in relevant trending conversations (don't spam, add value)
- Use video — it consistently outperforms static content in 2025–2026

## Measurement & Iteration

Track these metrics and adjust:
- **Reach**: Are people seeing your content? (algorithm health)
- **Engagement rate**: Likes + comments / reach (content quality signal)
- **Saves**: High saves = valuable content
- **Profile visits**: Are you attracting the right audience?
- **Link clicks**: Are you driving traffic to your site/trial?

Review monthly: what content types and hooks performed best? Double down on those.
