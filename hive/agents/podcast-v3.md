## 1. Starting a Podcast: The Definitive Checklist

### 1.1 Pre-Production: Before You Record

**Define Your Show's Purpose**
- Who is the listener? Be specific — not "marketers" but "B2B SaaS founders with 10-50 employees"
- What transformation does listening to your show create? (not "learn about marketing" but "make better decisions about their go-to-market strategy")
- How is this show differentiated from 3-4 similar shows they could be listening to instead?

**Show Format Options**

| Format | Description | Best For | Episodes/Month |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Interview** | Host + guest, guest answers questions | Thought leadership, networking | 2-4 |
| **Solo** | Host alone, sharing insights/opinions | Expertise establishment, authority | 4-8 |
| **Co-Hosted** | Two or more hosts in conversation | Entertainment, debate, banter | 2-4 |
| **Narrative/Documentary** | Storytelling with sound design | True crime, business stories | 1-2 |
| **News/Updates** | Short form, industry news | Busy professionals, newsletters | 8+ |

**Naming Your Podcast**
- Should be searchable, memorable, and hint at the topic
- Include keywords if SEO matters (e.g., "Marketing" in the title)
- Check Apple Podcasts and Spotify to ensure no direct competition with identical name
- Ideal length: 1-3 words for the show name + a clear subtitle
- Examples: "Acquired" (simple, memorable), "The Marketing Playbook" (keyword + topic), "How I Built This" (formula + aspiration)

### 1.2 Equipment: What You Actually Need

**Minimum Viable Podcast Setup ($100-200)**
- **Microphone**: Audio-Technica ATR2100x-USB (~$70) or Samson Q2U (~$60) — dynamic, USB + XLR
- **Headphones**: Any closed-back studio monitor; Sony MDR-7506 (~$100) is the industry standard
- **Pop filter**: $5-10 foam ball — plosives are your #1 audio quality killer
- **Free software**: Audacity (editing), Zoom or Riverside (recording)

**Recommended Podcast Setup ($300-500)**
- **Microphone**: Shure SM7B (~$400) with a Cloudlifter CL-1 inline preamp — the podcast industry standard
- **Interface**: Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 (~$150)
- **Acoustic treatment**: A reflection filter (~$100) or a closet full of clothes to dampen reflections
- **Software**: Descript (editing + transcription), Riverside or Zencastr (remote recording with local audio)

**Advanced Setup ($1,000+)**
- Sennheiser MKH 416 or Neumann U87 condenser mic
- Rodecaster Pro II all-in-one interface/mixer
- Dedicated soundproofed recording space
- Professional editing team (or high-end outsourced editing service)

**Submit Everything Through RSS**
- Your podcast host provides one RSS feed URL
- Submit that URL to every directory above
- When you publish new episodes, they pull automatically
- Directory listing approval can take 1-14 days (Apple is slowest)

### 1.4 The First 90 Days Launch Plan

**Week 1-2: Setup**
- [ ] Define show format and episode cadence (commit to a schedule you can keep)
- [ ] Design cover art (3000x3000px minimum, see Apple Podcasts specs)
- [ ] Write show description (150-character summary + 4,000-character detailed description)
- [ ] Record and edit pilot episode
- [ ] Set up hosting account and submit RSS to all directories

**Week 3-4: Pre-Launch Content**
- [ ] Write episode descriptions for first 3 episodes (write descriptions BEFORE recording — confirms episode topics)
- [ ] Record first 3 episodes (batch recording is more efficient than one-off)
- [ ] Create social media accounts for the show (@showhandle on all platforms)
- [ ] Write a "launch email" to your existing list announcing the show

**Week 5-6: Soft Launch**
- [ ] Submit to Apple Podcasts and Spotify
- [ ] Publish first episode
- [ ] Submit to all other directories
- [ ] Post on social media asking friends/clients to subscribe and leave a review

**Week 7-12: Growth Foundation**
- [ ] Publish consistently (every week or every two weeks — no matter what)
- [ ] Collect first 10 reviews (ask directly in the episode)
- [ ] Pitch to 5-10 existing podcasts as a guest
- [ ] Transcribe first 5 episodes (boosts SEO significantly)

## 2. Guesting on Podcasts

### 2.1 The Case for Podcast Guesting

Guesting on podcasts is one of the highest-leverage B2B marketing activities available:
- Direct access to an established, engaged audience (you skip the audience-building phase)
- Backlink from the show's website + social proof ("as seen on [Show]")
- Credibility by association — being on relevant podcasts positions you as an authority
- Relationship-building with the host (often leads to referrals, collaborations)
- High-quality link-building for SEO (podcast guesting backlinks are often easier to get than HARO)

### 2.2 Finding the Right Podcasts to Pitch

**Ideal Podcast Criteria**
- Audience alignment: Does this show's listener match your ideal customer profile?
- Size: Mid-size podcasts (500-5K downloads/episode) are often more receptive and have better audience engagement than massive shows
- Recency: When was the last episode published? (Inactive shows won't respond)
- Format fit: Can you add genuine value to their specific format?
- Host relationship: Do you have any connection to the host already?

**Pitching Strategies**

**Strategy 1: Direct Outreach**
- Find the podcast website → Contact/ Guest form
- Personalized pitch: reference a specific episode and what you found valuable
- Pitch your topic as a question ("Most SaaS founders struggle with X — I could break down 3 practical solutions")
- Attach or link to your own best content relevant to their audience

**Strategy 2: Podcast Guest Platforms**
- **Guestio** — podcast booking platform, you submit a profile and get booked
- **PodMatch** — match platform connecting podcasters with guests
- **PodcastGuests.com** — searchable database of shows accepting guests
- **HARO / Connectively** — some podcast producers use these to find guests

**Strategy 3: Warm Introductions**
- Ask mutual connections to intro you to podcast hosts
- This dramatically increases response rates vs. cold outreach

### 2.3 The Perfect Podcast Guest Appearance

**Pre-Recording Checklist**
- [ ] Research 3-5 episodes + understand the host's style and audience
- [ ] Prepare 3 potential topic angles (let the host pick)
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 specific stories/examples (NOT just "tips")
- [ ] Test audio: headphones, mic, quiet space, stable internet (for remote)
- [ ] Have water nearby — your voice sounds better when you're hydrated
- [ ] Close all unnecessary apps on your computer
- [ ] Have a "cheat sheet" open — topic bullets, stats, story outlines

**During the Recording**
- **Be 5 minutes early** — technical issues eat into recording time
- **Match the host's energy** — if they're casual, be casual; if professional, be professional
- **Answer the question, THEN add depth** — never ignore what was asked
- **Use specifics over generics** — "We grew from $2K to $40K MRR in 8 months" beats "we had good growth"
- **Don't sell** — give value so generously that buying from you becomes the obvious next step
- **Reference the host's work** — shows you've done homework and builds rapport
- **Promote one thing at the end** — one CTA max, tied to something genuinely useful

**Post-Recording**
- Thank the host immediately with a personalized note
- Provide a link or resource they mentioned in the show
- Share the episode on your social channels when it goes live
- Add the appearance to your website, LinkedIn, and email signature
- Add the show to your media kit as a credibility credential

## 3. Repurposing Podcast Content

### 3.1 The Repurposing Framework

One hour of podcast recording should generate 10-15 pieces of content across 5-8 formats. The key is to work top-down: record once, extract highest-value content first.

**The Content Ladder**
```
Episode Recording
  ↓ Audio
Full Episode MP3 → podcast host
  ↓ Transcribe
Full Transcript (.txt / .srt)
  ↓ Extract & Repurpose
YouTube Video (async, slides + audio)
  ↓ Clip
Short Clips (1-3 min) → TikTok / Reels / Shorts
  ↓ Write
LinkedIn Article (from transcript)
  ↓ Extract
Twitter/X Threads (from key quotes + takeaways)
  ↓ Format
Show Notes (blog post + key links)
  ↓ Design
Quote Graphics (for social media)
  ↓ Email
Email Newsletter Section (teaser + link to full episode)
  ↓ Bonus
Guest Bio + Headshot → website media page
```

### 3.2 Tools for Repurposing at Scale

| Output Format | Primary Tool | Workflow |
|---|---|---|
| **Audio transcription** | Riverside (built-in), Rev, Descript | Auto-generate → human review |
| **YouTube video** | Riverside (records video), Camtasia | Edit in Riverside or Camtasia |
| **Short clips** | Opus Clip, Vidyo.ai, Kapwing | AI clips from full video |
| **LinkedIn articles** | Manual (from transcript) | Copy + format + add CTA |
| **Twitter/X threads** | Manual or Claude (help) | Extract 5-8 takeaways |
| **Quote graphics** | Canva | Pull 5-7 quotes + add design |
| **Newsletter** | Mailchimp / Beehiiv | Teaser + link to show notes |

### 3.3 YouTube Optimization for Podcasters

**Why Upload to YouTube?**
- YouTube is the world's second-largest search engine
- Your podcast is audio-only on most platforms — YouTube adds the visual dimension
- YouTube videos rank in Google search results alongside web content
- Short clips from YouTube can be repurposed for YouTube Shorts + TikTok + Reels

**YouTube Best Practices for Podcasters**
- Use podcast cover art as thumbnail (add text overlay: "Episode Title")
- Upload 60-90 second clips to YouTube Shorts — high discoverability
- Add chapters (timestamps) to long-form episodes — improves watch time and SEO
- Use keywords in title, description, and tags
- End screen: embed/link to subscribe and full episode
- Upload the full episode with podcast cover art + waveform overlay (Descript can do this)

### 3.4 Short-Form Clip Strategy

**What Makes a Great Clip**
- Stand-alone value: Someone who watches only this clip gets value
- Strong hook in first 3 seconds (start at the most compelling moment)
- Ends with a hook to the full episode ("For the full interview, link in bio")
- Captions always (95% of people watch without sound)
- Vertical format (9:16) for TikTok/Reels/Shorts

**Clip Extraction Workflow**
1. Listen to full episode and mark 5-7 timestamps of strong moments
2. In Descript or Opus Clip, extract clips around those timestamps (30-90 seconds)
3. Add captions in Descript or Kapwing
4. Create a template in Canva for branded quote graphics
5. Publish to TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and LinkedIn Video

## 4. Audio Editing Workflows

### 4.1 Audacity Workflow

**Why Audacity Still Matters in 2026**
- Completely free, open-source
- Runs on Mac, Windows, and Linux
- Huge community + extensive tutorial library
- Sufficient for basic-to-intermediate editing without subscription cost

**Core Audacity Workflow**
```
1. Import audio → File > Import > Audio
2. Noise Reduction:
   - Select 2-3 seconds of "silence" where only room noise is present
   - Effect > Noise Reduction > "Take Noise Profile"
   - Select all audio > Effect > Noise Reduction
   - Settings: Noise Reduction (dB) = 12, Sensitivity = 6, Frequency Smoothing = 3
3. EQ / Voice Enhancement:
   - Effect > Filter Curve EQ
   - Cut below 80Hz (removes rumble)
   - Boost 200-300Hz slightly (adds warmth to voice)
   - Cut around 2-4kHz slightly (reduces harshness)
4. Compression:
   - Effect > Compressor
   - Threshold: -18dB, Ratio: 3:1, Attack: 0.2s, Release: 1.0s
5. Normalize:
   - Effect > Normalize (peak amplitude: -1.0dB)
6. Export:
   - File > Export > Export as MP3
   - Bit Rate: 128kbps for speech (48kbps too low, 320kbps too high)
```

**Audacity Plugins to Install**
- **Chris's Dynamic Effects** — better compression and limiting
- **Clarity Vx** (paid plugin) — AI-powered noise reduction and voice isolation
- **Loudness Penalty** — checks how your audio will sound on different platforms

### 4.2 Descript Workflow (Recommended for Most Podcasters)

**Why Descript Changed the Game**
- Edit audio/video by editing the transcript — like a Google Doc
- AI-generated, editable transcript included with paid plans
- Automatic filler word removal ("um," "uh," "like")
- One-click removal of all silence
- Built-in screen recording + YouTube export
- Social clip creation without leaving the app

**Descript Core Workflow**
```
1. Record or import audio/video
2. Review auto-transcript:
   - Correct any obvious errors (names, product names, industry terms)
   - Add speaker labels if multi-person
3. Edit by editing transcript:
   - Cut filler words: click "um"/"uh" → Delete
   - Cut sentences: select → Delete
   - Reorder: drag and drop paragraphs
4. Studio Sound: click "Studio Sound" (AI noise reduction + voice enhancement)
5. Remove silence: Filler > Remove All Silence
6. Export:
   - Podcast: "Export" → MP3/WAV
   - Video: "Export" → MP4
   - YouTube: "Publish to YouTube" with SEO metadata
7. Social clips: "Create clips" → select segments → add captions → download
```

**Descript Pricing Notes (2026)**
- **Free**: 1 hour of transcription, 3 exports/month (good for trying)
- **Creator ($12/mo)**: Unlimited transcription + exports, Studio Sound
- **Pro ($30/mo)**: Multi-track editing, better AI features, team collaboration
- **Enterprise**: Custom pricing

### 4.3 Recommended Export Settings

| Platform | Format | Bitrate | Sample Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Podcast (Apple/Spotify)** | MP3 | 128kbps (min), 192kbps (recommended) | 44.1kHz |
| **YouTube** | MP4 (H.264) | N/A (video) | 44.1kHz audio |
| **Short-form video** | MP4 | N/A (video) | 44.1kHz audio |
| **Email/newsletter embed** | MP3 | 64-96kbps | 44.1kHz |

## 5. Podcast SEO

### 5.1 Why Podcast SEO Matters

Most podcast discoverability comes from:
1. Apple Podcasts / Spotify search (keyword-based)
2. Recommendation algorithms (behavioral)
3. Social shares and direct links
4. Google search (increasingly important since Google indexes podcast content)

**Podcast SEO = Optimizing for Discovery Across All Platforms**

### 5.2 Show-Level SEO

**Title Optimization**
- Primary keyword in the show name (e.g., "The SaaS Podcast" for "SaaS podcast" search)
- Clear enough for humans, keyword-optimized enough for algorithms
- Keep under 30 characters for the main title; subtitle handles the rest

**Description Optimization**
- First 150-200 characters appear in Apple Podcasts search results — make them count
- Front-load keywords naturally in the first paragraph
- Include: what the show is, who it's for, what topics are covered
- Mention 5-10 specific topics/episodes to signal content breadth

**Categories**
- Apple Podcasts allows 1 primary + 2 secondary categories
- Choose the most specific applicable category (niche beats broad)
- Example for B2B SaaS show: Primary = "Business / Marketing", Secondary = "Technology"

### 5.3 Episode-Level SEO

**Episode Titles**
- Front-load the value or topic: "Ep 247: How to Fix Cold Email Deliverability" over "A Chat with Jane Smith"
- Include the guest name if they have a following: "Ep 247: Jane Smith on Cold Email Deliverability"

**Show Notes Optimization**
- Write 150-300 words of substantive show notes (not "we talked about X and Y")
- Include timestamps (signals structure to search engines)
- Mention topics, tools, and guest by name (backlinks where possible)
- Include full transcript links

**Transcript-Based SEO**
- Publish transcript on your website as a blog post
- Use headings (H2, H3) matching the questions discussed
- Internal link to related episodes
- Target long-tail keywords in the transcript text

### 5.4 Website Integration for Podcast SEO

**Create a Podcast-Specific Page Structure**
```
/podcast (show homepage)
  ├── /podcast/episode-001 (per-episode show notes pages)
  ├── /podcast/guests (guest directory page)
  └── /podcast/subscribe (RSS + directory subscription links)
```

**Schema Markup for Podcast Pages**
- Use Google's Podcast schema (Google Podcast markup)
- Add `PodcastSeries` schema to show homepage
- Add `PodcastEpisode` schema to each episode page

## 6. Building Podcast Audience Community

### 6.1 Why Community Matters More Than Downloads

Downloads are a vanity metric. A community of 500 highly engaged listeners who share episodes, buy products, and refer others is more valuable than 50,000 passive downloaders who never engage.

### 6.2 Community Building Strategies

**Strategy 1: The Listener Lounge (Private Community)**
- Create a private Slack, Discord, or Circle community
- Promote it at the end of every episode: "Join 2,000 podcast listeners in the [Show Name] community"
- Offer exclusive content: episode transcripts, early access to episodes, Q&A with guests
- Gate it with an email signup or a simple approval process

**Strategy 2: Audience Participation Content**
- Weekly "voicemails" or voice messages from listeners (play on the show)
- Monthly AMA (Ask Me Anything) episodes
- Polls via social media with results shared in the show
- Listener-submitted questions for guests (gives audience a stake in the show)

**Strategy 3: Consistent Listener Recognition**
- "Shout out" listeners who share episodes, leave reviews, or send feedback
- Create a "regular listener" identity — listeners who tune in every episode feel part of something
- Call out milestones publicly ("We just hit 1,000 reviews — you're incredible")

**Strategy 4: Convert Listeners to Advocates**
- Make it frictionless to share: create pre-written social copy for each episode
- Provide shareable graphics (quote cards, episode thumbnails)
- Create a "listener referral program": earn something when a friend subscribes

### 6.3 Measurement: What to Track Beyond Downloads

| Metric | Why It Matters | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **5-star review rate** | Sign of true fan love | Steady increase over time |
| **Social mentions/tagged posts** | Organic advocacy signal | 2-5 per episode minimum |
| **Email list growth from podcast** | Converts audience to owned channel | 1-3% of monthly downloads |
| **Community join rate** | Commitment to the show | 5-10% of downloads per episode |
| **Episode completion rate** | Are they listening to the end? | >65% is good for long-form |
| **Guest-forward referrals** | Guests recommending you to other guests | Track per guest |

## 7. Key Takeaways & Action Items

### Immediate Priorities (This Week)
1. **Decide on podcast format and recording cadence** — commit to one schedule
2. **Set up recording infrastructure** (mic, headphones, Descript or Audacity)
3. **Create cover art** (3000x3000px, compliant with Apple Podcasts specs)
4. **Claim your podcast's show notes page on your website** (SEO foundation)

### Medium-Term (30-60 Days)
5. **Launch your first 4 episodes** — batch record, then release weekly
6. **Submit RSS to all major podcast directories** — don't skip Stitcher, iHeartRadio
7. **Create a repurposing workflow** — map every episode to 5 content outputs
8. **Build a list of 20 target podcasts to guest on** — start with warm intros where possible
9. **Set up a simple listener community** (Slack/Discord) with exclusive access

### Long-Term (Quarterly)
10. **Track episode completion rate** — optimize content for retention
11. **Build a guest booking pipeline** — aim to book 1-2 guests/month consistently
12. **Transcribe your top 10 episodes** and publish as blog posts for SEO
13. **Do a quarterly review**: downloads trend, review rate, community growth, revenue attribution
