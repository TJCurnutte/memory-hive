# Conversion Rate Optimization — B2B Sales Tactics

## Handling Objections

### "It's too expensive"
- Reframe value, not price: "What would it cost to *not* solve this problem?"
- Use ROI calculators or case study data
- Offer payment terms or phased implementation
- Compare to competitor costs or status quo costs

### "We're not ready / no budget"
- Qualify urgency: "If you were ready, would this be a priority?"
- Plant seeds for next quarter: "What would need to change for this to make sense?"
- Offer low-commitment next steps (pilot, trial, assessment)

### "We need to think about it / talk to the team"
- Agree, then accelerate: "Totally makes sense. What do you think will be the hardest sell internally?"
- Offer to join the internal conversation
- Send pre-formed objection-handling one-pagers for them to share

### "We're already working with a competitor"
- Focus on differentiation, not disparagement
- "What would need to be true for you to consider alternatives?"
- Position as a supplement, not replacement (if applicable)

### "This isn't a priority right now"
- Surface urgency: "What IS the priority? When does this become urgent?"
- Connect to their stated goals or external forces (regulation, market shift)
- Offer to reconnect at a defined future date

## Urgency Creation (Ethical)

- **Limited availability**: "I only have 2 slots left this quarter"
- **Price increase notice**: "Pricing goes up on [date]"
- **Case for urgency**: Tie to their own stated timeline or business cycles
- **Scarcity of value**: "This program only runs twice a year"
- **Avoid**: Fake deadlines, artificial pressure — erodes trust

## Social Proof Mechanisms

### Client Logos
- Display prominently on proposals, emails, website
- Especially logos of recognizable or similar-industry companies

### Case Studies
- Structure: Problem → Approach → Results (with specific metrics)
- 3–5 case studies covering different use cases/industries
- Short 1-page executive summaries + full versions

### Testimonials
- Collect via NPS survey or post-onboarding feedback
- Video testimonials are highest-credibility format
- Attribute fully: Name, Title, Company

### Industry Associations / Certifications
- "Trusted by X companies in Y industry"
- Security/compliance badges (SOC2, HIPAA, etc.)

### Peer Influence
- "Other [title]s in your industry have found..."
- Share anonymized peer conversations: "We heard the same concern from..."

## Closing Techniques

### Assumptive Close
- "Let's get this started Monday / I'll send the contract now"
- Use after verbal agreement, not before

### Summary Close
- Recap agreed value before asking for the next step
- "So to make sure we're aligned — you're looking for X, Y is the biggest priority, and you'd like to get started by [date]?"

### Urgency Close
- Pair with real constraint: "Given our production schedule, we'd need to start by [date] to hit that for you"

### Next-Step Close
- Always have a clear, specific next step with date/time
- "Would Tuesday at 2pm work for the kickoff call?"

### Champion Close
- Arm your internal champion: give them tools to sell internally
- Provide ROI calc, one-pager, competitive comparison they can share

## Identifying Buying Signals

### Verbal Cues
- "How would this work if we..."
- "When could we get started?"
- Detailed questions about pricing, implementation, support
- "What if we wanted to..." (scope expansion)

### Behavioral Cues
- Requesting meeting with multiple stakeholders
- Sharing with internal team (CC'ing others, looping in procurement)
- Visiting pricing page after a demo
- Comparing you to alternatives actively

## Conversion Rate Benchmarks (B2B SaaS)
- Lead-to-MQL: 5–15%
- MQL-to-SQL: 20–30%
- SQL-to-Opportunity: 50–60%
- Opportunity-to-Close: 20–30%
- *Source: Gartner, TOPO, HubSpot benchmarks*
