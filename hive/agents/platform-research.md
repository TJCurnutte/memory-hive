| Day | Best Time Window |
|-----|------------------|
| Tuesday–Thursday | 8–10 AM EST, 12–2 PM EST |

### Post Frequency

## X/Twitter — Content Formats That Win

### 1. **Text Posts (Vanilla Tweets)**
- Best for: quick takes, opinions, one-liners, replies
- Length: 100–200 chars performs well (leaves room for replies)
- Hooks that work: contrarian takes, "unpopular opinion:" starts, numbers/statements, bold claims
- Examples of hooks:
  - "The thing nobody talks about..."
  - "I spent 3 months doing X. Here's what I learned."
  - "Hot take:"
  - "The biggest mistake most people make with [X]..."
  - "POV:"

### 2. **Threads**
- Best for: tutorials, breakdowns, storytelling, deep dives
- Optimal length: 8–15 tweets (shorter than you think)
- First tweet is everything — must hook in the first 1–2 lines
- Include a hook tweet at the start, summarize at the end
- Must end with an engagement prompt (question, "drop a 🧵 if...")

### 3. **Carousels (PDF/Images)**
- 3–6 slides work best
- Strong for: tips, frameworks, step-by-step guides
- First slide must stand alone as a hook
- Use contrast, bold text, visual hierarchy
- Best performing carousel topics: productivity, growth tactics, industry frameworks, tool comparisons

### 4. **Short-form Video (Twitter/X Video)**
- 30–90 seconds works best
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- Auto-captions required (most watch muted)
- Good for: demos, behind-the-scenes, quick tutorials, hot takes

### 5. **Polls**
- High engagement, good for community building
- Ask binary or simple multiple choice
- Great for: opinions, predictions, product direction
- Tip: polls keep your account visible for 24–48 hours

### 6. **Quote Tweets with Commentary**
- Taking a tweet and adding 2–3 sentences of commentary = high engagement
- Works well for industry news, competitor takes, viral posts

## X/Twitter — Engagement Patterns

### What Drives REPLIES:
- Asking questions
- Controversial/unpopular opinions
- Take requests ("Agree or disagree?")
- Relatable content (day-in-the-life, struggles)

### What Drives REPOSTS:
- Useful information people want their network to see
- Strong opinions/hot takes
- Infographics and carousels
- News/commentary on current events

### What Drives SAVES:
- Educational content (tutorials, templates, frameworks)
- Resources (tools, reading lists, templates)
- Deeply insightful threads

### Best Practices:
- Always end with a call-to-action or question
- Respond to replies within first 30 minutes to boost algorithm
- Engage with others' content to build reciprocity
- Pin one tweet that acts as a permanent intro/CTA

## LinkedIn — Content Formats That Win

### 1. **Text Posts (Thought Leadership)**
- Conversational tone performs better than corporate
- Mix of: personal stories + professional lessons
- Use line breaks (empty line between paragraphs = readability)
- Strong opening line is critical — no "In today's article..."

### 2. **Carousels**
- 8–15 slides, landscape (1080x1080 or 1080x1350)
- Educational content: tips, frameworks, case studies
- Must have a clear "hook slide" and a "takeaway slide"
- End with clear CTA

### 3. **Short-form Video**
- 30–90 seconds for LinkedIn
- Talking head + screen recording hybrid works well
- Hook: "I'm going to show you how to..." / "Here's what most people get wrong..."
- Auto-captions required

### 4. **Documents (Native PDFs)**
- Often outperform image carousels
- Great for: step-by-step guides, playbooks, templates

### 5. **Articles**
- Use sparingly — algorithm favors posts over articles now
- Good for: long-form thought leadership, announcements

## LinkedIn — Hook Patterns

1. **Personal stories**: "I made this mistake, and it cost me $X" / "A founder told me something last week that..."
2. **Controversial takes**: "The advice to [X] is wrong" / "Nobody talks about the real reason..."

4. **Questions**: "Has anyone else noticed...?" / "What's your take on...?"
5. **Bold statements**: "This is the most underrated [X]" / "[X] is broken, here's why"

## LinkedIn — Engagement Patterns

### What Drives Comments:
- Controversial/opinion posts
- Asking for reader's experience
- "Drop your answer below" type prompts
- Sharing a personal failure/mistake

### What Drives Shares:
- Useful resources, templates, toolkits
- Industry insights and trends
- Career advice, leadership content
- Personal achievements and milestones

### What Drives Saves:
- In-depth guides and frameworks
- Templates and resources
- Detailed breakdowns of processes

### Best Practices:
- 3–5 posts per week for active presence
- Respond to all comments in first hour
- Tag relevant people to increase reach
- First comment (as OP) should add additional value

## Key General Principles

1. **Consistency beats perfection** — post regularly regardless of ideal timing
2. **Test and iterate** — what works varies by audience; measure your own analytics
3. **Hook first** — no matter the format, the first 1–2 lines determine click-through
4. **Engage to grow** — reply to others, comment genuinely, build relationships
5. **Repurpose** — one long-form piece can become 10+ posts across both platforms
