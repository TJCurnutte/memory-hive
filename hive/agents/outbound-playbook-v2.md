### SKILL 1: PROSPECTING

**Definition:** The systematic identification and qualification of potential customers (prospects) for your product or service. The foundation of all outbound revenue.

**Core Tactics:**
- Define ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) across firmographics: industry, sub-industry, company size, revenue range, tech stack, geography
- Leverage data providers: Apollo.io, ZoomInfo, Clearbit, LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Build Target Account Lists (TALs) in waves of 50-100 accounts
- Research accounts before outreach: funding news, leadership changes, press releases, job postings
- Identify buyers within accounts: Economic Buyer, Champion, User Buyer, Technical Evaluator
- Track trigger events: new funding, C-suite hires, product launches, expansion announcements
- Maintain CRM hygiene: log every activity, update status, move leads through stages

**Frameworks:**
- **ICP Fit Score:** 1-10 rating based on firmographic + technographic + behavioral fit
- **TAM/SAM/SOM:** Total Addressable Market → Serviceable Available Market → Serviceable Obtainable Market
- **Account Tiering:** Tier 1 (perfect fit, high intent), Tier 2 (good fit), Tier 3 (possible)
- **White Space Analysis:** Find gaps in competitor accounts you can displace
- **Account Research Score:** Rate each account 1-10 on: pain fit, authority access, timing, budget signals

**KPIs:** Accounts targeted/week, Contacts identified/account, New leads created/week, Prospecting time/day

### SKILL 2: PERSONALIZATION AT SCALE

**Definition:** Creating the perception of 1:1 custom outreach while leveraging tools, data, and AI to operate at scale. The single biggest lever on reply rates.

**Core Tactics:**
- Use **dynamic variable insertion** (first name, company, role, industry) as baseline
- Layer in **trigger-based personalization:** recent news, job changes, LinkedIn posts, conference presence
- Create **account-specific openers** based on deep research before sending
- Build **modular email templates** with personalized blocks that swap based on ICP tier
- Use AI (Claude/ChatGPT) to generate personalized email drafts from research notes
- Create custom **video snippets** (Loom, Vidyard) that address specific use cases
- Build **personalized landing pages** for target accounts
- Reference mutual connections or shared experiences (LinkedIn groups, events, alumni networks)
- Add industry-specific pain statements only someone in that role would recognize

**Personalization Variables (Ranked by Impact):**

| Rank | Variable | Impact | Example |
|------|----------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Recent news about their company | Highest | "[Company] just raised $50M..." |
| 2 | Their LinkedIn post or comment | High | "Your post on [topic] was insightful..." |
| 3 | Job change or new hire | High | "Congrats on the new [role]..." |
| 4 | Industry-specific pain + solution | Medium-High | "Most CFOs are dealing with..." |
| 5 | Company size/revenue benchmark | Medium | "Companies like yours with 50-200 employees..." |
| 6 | Role-specific challenge | Medium | "As a VP of Sales, you're likely dealing with..." |
| 7 | Mutual connection or referral | Very High | "Saw we're both connected to [Name]..." |
| 8 | Shared event or conference | Medium | "Saw you were at [Conference] too..." |
| 9 | Company tech stack overlap | Medium | "I noticed you use [Tool] — we integrate with it..." |
| 10 | First name + company name only | Lowest | Table stakes only — not sufficient |

**Tools:** Clay, Apollo, Clearbit, ZoomInfo, ChatGPT, Claude, Smartlead, Instantly, Regie.ai

### SKILL 3: MULTI-CHANNEL OUTREACH

**Definition:** Engaging prospects across two or more channels (email, LinkedIn, phone, social) in a coordinated sequence to maximize touchpoints and response rates. The modern standard for outbound.

**Channel Strategy by Role:**

| Role | Primary Channel | Secondary Channel | Tertiary |
|------|----------------|-------------------|----------|
| C-Suite / VP | LinkedIn DM, Direct Email | Cold Call/VM | Twitter/X DM |
| Director / Manager | Email, LinkedIn DM | Cold Call | SMS |
| Individual Contributor | Email | LinkedIn InMail | Cold Call |
| Technical / Engineering | Email (technical framing) | LinkedIn | GitHub/community |

**Multi-Channel Sequence Design Principles:**
- **Spacing:** 2-5 days between touches on same channel
- **Escalation:** Email → LinkedIn → Call → Escalation (different angle)
- **Standalone messages:** Each touch must make sense without context from other touches
- **Value-first:** Every touch adds value, not just "following up"
- **Single CTA:** One clear action per message
- **Channel match:** Tone and content should match channel norms

**Optimal 5-Touch Multi-Channel Sequence:**

| Touch | Channel | Day | Content |
|-------|---------|-----|---------|
| 1 | LinkedIn Connection Request | Day 0 | Warm, no pitch, reference their content |
| 2 | LinkedIn DM / InMail | Day 2 | Personalized hook + soft CTA |
| 3 | Cold Email | Day 4 | Curiosity hook, tailored to their context |
| 4 | Cold Call + Voicemail | Day 6 | Callback number, brief value prop |
| 5 | LinkedIn Post Reply / Comment | Day 9 | Engage their content, re-engage |

### SKILL 4: LINKEDIN MESSAGING

**Definition:** Using LinkedIn's social graph and messaging tools to initiate relationships, build credibility, and advance deals through professional social engagement.

**Connection Requests:**
- **Personalized note (>40 chars):** Response rate 40-60% vs. 20% for "I'd like to connect"
- Hook: Reference something specific they've posted, published, or are involved in
- Keep under 300 characters (LinkedIn limit for connection note)
- Template: "Hi [Name], loved your post on [topic] — the point about [specific insight] is something most [their industry] teams miss. Would love to connect and compare notes."

**LinkedIn InMail (Premium):**
- Use strategically for Tier 1 accounts only
- Subject line = subject of their own content (highest opens)
- Max 200 words — shorter = higher reply rates
- Structure: Hook → Personalization → Value prop → Soft CTA

**LinkedIn DM (2nd-degree connections — no character limit):**
- Warm opener referencing mutual connection or shared content
- Can include links but lead with value
- Respond to their recent content within 24 hours

**LinkedIn Automation Tools:** Phantombuster, Dux-Soup, Salesflow, Expandi, MeetAlfred

**Best Practices:**
- Post on LinkedIn 3x/week minimum to fuel social selling engine
- Comment on prospects' posts (not just Like) — triggers notification + visibility
- Share relevant content that prospects would find valuable
- Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator for advanced search + account alerting

### SKILL 5: EMAIL SEQUENCES

**Definition:** A series of timed, triggered emails sent to prospects designed to warm, qualify, and convert them across the buyer journey.

**Sequence Architecture:**
- **Entry trigger:** Added by action (form fill, manual add, data list upload)
- **Exit triggers:** Reply, book, opt-out, bounce, no-reply after max touches
- **Timing:** Configurable — typical cadence 2-5 days between emails
- **Personalization layers:** Static (name/company) + Dynamic (trigger events)

**Email Copywriting Frameworks:**

**AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action):**
- Subject: Attention-grabbing opener
- Body: Build interest through pain/curiosity
- Desire: Show how you solve it
- CTA: Clear, single action

**PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution):**
- Identify the problem
- Agitate it (make it worse/more urgent)
- Present your solution

**The 4P Formula (Cold Email):**
- **Picture:** Paint a vivid picture of their current situation
- **Promise:** State the transformation you offer
- **Proof:** Social proof or data backing your claim
- **Push:** Call to action

**Subject Line Tactics:**
- Numbers: "3 questions about [Company]" (+22% opens)
- Questions: "Is [Company] still using [old tool]?" (+18% opens)
- Curiosity: "Quick question about your [process]"
- Specificity: "[Founder name]'s company just did [X] — curious about [Company]"
- NO click-bait, no ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation, no emojis

**Email Length by Stage:**
- Cold outreach: 50-100 words
- Follow-up: 25-50 words (shorter = better)
- Nurture: 150-300 words (more context needed)

### SKILL 6: OBJECTION HANDLING

**Definition:** Responding effectively to prospect concerns, pushbacks, and rejections to maintain relationship momentum and advance the sale without losing credibility.

**The CORE Framework for Objections:**
- **Clarify:** "Can you help me understand more about that?"
- **Own:** Validate the concern — "That's a fair point, and..."
- **Rebut:** Provide evidence/data/reframe
- **Engage:** Pivot to next step or question

**Top Objections + Full Responses:**

**"We're not interested right now"**
→ "Totally understand. What would need to be true for this to become a priority? I just want to make sure I'm timing this right."

**"We don't have budget"**
→ "Budget is almost always a prioritization question, not a capability one. If this could save you [X hours/week / $Y per month], would that be worth exploring?"

**"Send me more information" (the brush-off)**
→ "Happy to. What specific questions should I focus on in the deck? That way it's tailored to what you're actually trying to solve."

**"We're already working with [Competitor]"**
→ "That's great — most teams are. The question is whether [Competitor] is actually delivering on [specific metric]. Would it be worth 15 minutes to benchmark?"

**"This isn't a priority"**
→ "Completely understand. What would make this a priority? Is it just about timing, or are there other factors at play?"

**"I need to talk to my team/manager"**
→ "Absolutely. What would you need to present to them to make the case? I can put together something specifically for that conversation."

**"I'm too busy"**
→ "I completely respect that. Would [10 min] work better? Or if there's someone else who owns this area, I'm happy to loop them in."

**"Your price is too high"**
→ "Let's separate the conversation. Price and value — I'd love to understand what value would make this a no-brainer. Then we can talk numbers."

### SKILL 7: QUALIFICATION FRAMEWORKS

**Definition:** Structured criteria used to evaluate whether a prospect is worth pursuing (qualifying in) or should be deprioritized (qualifying out). The goal: spend time on the right deals.

#### BANT (Classic — IBM)
- **Budget:** Do they have the financial resources?
- **Authority:** Can they make/influence the decision?
- **Need:** Do they have a genuine problem you can solve?
- **Timeline:** When are they looking to solve it?

#### MEDDIC (High-Tech / Enterprise)
- **Metrics:** What quantitative outcome will they achieve?
- **Economic Buyer:** Who controls the budget?
- **Decision Criteria:** What are they using to evaluate solutions?
- **Decision Process:** How do they actually buy?
- **Identify Pain:** What is the cost of the problem?
- **Champion:** Do you have an internal advocate?

#### CHAMP (Modern Evolution)
- **Challenges:** What specific problems are they facing?
- **Authority:** Who else is involved in the decision?
- **Money:** What's the budget and what's the ROI?
- **Prioritization:** Why now vs. later?

#### ANUM (Simple / SMB)
- **Authority:** Who do you need to talk to?
- **Need:** Do they actually need what you offer?
- **Urgency:** When do they need it?
- **Money:** Can they afford it?

#### SPIN Selling
- **Situation:** Understand current state
- **Problem:** Identify pain points
- **Implication:** Explore cost/impact of not solving
- **Need-Payoff:** Get them to articulate the value of solving

**Qualification Checkpoint Questions:**
1. "What's the biggest challenge you're facing right now?" (Need)
2. "Who else is involved in this decision?" (Authority)
3. "What's your timeline for solving this?" (Urgency)
4. "What's the budget for this initiative?" (Money)
5. "What would have to be true for this to be a success?" (Success criteria)

### SKILL 8: COLD CALLING

**Definition:** Telephone outreach to prospects who have not yet engaged, used to interrupt their day with enough value to secure a conversation.

**Cold Call Structure (60 seconds):**
1. **Opener (10 sec):** "Hi [Name], this is [Your name] with [Company]. I'm calling because [personalization hook]. Do you have 90 seconds?"
2. **Hook (20 sec):** State the problem/challenge you solve — specific to their role/industry
3. **Value prop (15 sec):** One-sentence outcome: "We help [ICP] to [achieve X outcome]"
4. **Ask (10 sec):** "Would it be worth 15 minutes to explore whether this applies to [Company]?"
5. **Close for time:** "What does your calendar look like [tomorrow/this week]?"

**Best Practices:**
- Call between 8-10am or 4-6pm local time (highest connect rates)
- Leave voicemails — they reactivate prospects: "Hi [Name], [personalized hook], I'll try you again [day]. Best number is [your number]."
- Use Voicemail drop tools (Callwagon, YouMail) for scale
- Track connect rate, voicemail rate, conversion to meeting
- Gatekeepers: "I'm a vendor calling on behalf of [CEO/exec name]" often works
- Always verify you're reaching the right person — if not, ask for the right contact

**Key Metrics:** Calls made/day, Connects, Meetings booked, Talk time, Voicemail rate

### SKILL 9: TERRITORY PLANNING

**Definition:** Designing a geographic, industry, or account-based territory to maximize SDR efficiency and coverage.

**Steps:**
1. Define ICP — who are your best customers historically?
2. Build account universe using data providers (Apollo, ZoomInfo)
3. Segment by: Industry, Size, Geography, Tech stack, Intent signals
4. Assign accounts to reps based on capacity (100-200 accounts/rep typical)
5. Set coverage plan: # contacts per account, # touches planned
6. Review and adjust quarterly based on conversion data

**Account Coverage Models:**
- **Named Account:** Full ABM treatment — dedicated outreach to all key stakeholders
- **Hunter/Farmer:** SDRs hunt new logos, AEs farm existing accounts
- **Land & Expand:** Target accounts that can grow over time

### SKILL 10: ICP (IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE) DEFINITION

**Definition:** A precise description of the companies and contacts most likely to buy from you and get the most value from your product.

**ICP Components:**
- **Firmographics:** Industry, sub-industry, company size, employee count, revenue range, geography
- **Technographics:** Current tech stack, tools used, competitive customer overlaps
- **Intent signals:** Actively searching for your solution category (via 3rd-party intent data)
- **Behavioral:** Website visits, content downloads, email engagement, demo requests
- **Psychographics:** Buyer priorities, decision-making style, risk tolerance

**How to Build ICP:**
1. Analyze 20 best historical customers — what do they have in common?
2. Analyze churned customers — what red flags existed?
3. Interview AEs and CSMs about best-fit vs. worst-fit accounts
4. Score accounts against ICP attributes
5. Test ICP on new data — do ideal accounts convert at higher rates?

### SKILL 11: CRM HYGIENE

**Definition:** Maintaining accurate, complete, and up-to-date records in the CRM to ensure pipeline visibility, forecasting accuracy, and revenue intelligence.

**Daily CRM Discipline:**
- Log every customer interaction within 24 hours
- Update lead status after each touch (Contacted, Qualified, Nurturing, Dead)
- Log call notes immediately (not end of day)
- Update fields: next step, next step date, owner, deal value estimate
- Close lost opportunities with reason codes (Not interested, No budget, Competitor, Timing)

**CRM Workflows:**
- Lead routing: Auto-assign new leads to SDRs by round-robin or ICP match
- Follow-up alerts: Task/activity created automatically when no response
- Stage progression: Auto-advance leads based on activity (reply, meeting booked)
- Deduplication: Merge duplicate contacts/companies

**Best Tools:** Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, Zoho CRM, Close CRM

### SKILL 14: FOLLOW-UP CADENCES

**Definition:** The art and science of re-engaging prospects who have not responded to initial outreach, using timing, channel shifts, and content variations to maximize conversion.

**The Rule of 7:** Prospects need an average of 7-13 touches before converting. Most SDRs quit after 1-2. The money is in the follow-up.

**Follow-Up Best Practices:**
- Follow up 3-5x minimum before marking a lead as "exhausted"
- Change channel on each follow-up: Email → LinkedIn → Call → Different email angle
- Use new information as re-engagement hook: "Saw you just [event]..."
- Shorten messages on each follow-up (prospects who didn't respond want less to read)
- Escalate urgency/curiosity on each follow-up
- Time follows based on engagement: reply to last message within 2 hours; no-reply gets 3-5 day gap

**Re-engagement Scripts:**
- New content: "Saw your post on [topic] — it reminded me of our conversation about [pain]. Still relevant?"
- Timing shift: "I know [day] tends to be busy — wanted to try you on [different day]"
- New angle: "Quick update — we just launched [feature] that specifically addresses [their previous concern]"
- Social proof: "[Company similar to theirs] just achieved [outcome]. Worth revisiting?"

**Cadence Structure Example (8-touch):**
1. Day 0: LinkedIn Connection Request
2. Day 2: LinkedIn DM
3. Day 4: Cold Email #1
4. Day 7: Cold Email #2 (different angle)
5. Day 10: Cold Call + Voicemail
6. Day 14: LinkedIn DM #2 (comment on their post)
7. Day 18: Cold Email #3 (social proof + new hook)
8. Day 25: Final email (breakup email / softer approach)

### SKILL 15: GATEKEEPER STRATEGIES

**Definition:** Techniques for navigating past administrative assistants, receptionists, and other "screens" to reach the actual decision-maker.

**Gatekeeper Bypass Tactics:**
- Use the executive's name confidently: "I'm calling for [CEO name]"
- Frame as existing relationship: "I'm working with [VP name] on a project"
- Leave a specific callback number: "Can you have [name] call me at [number]? It's regarding [specific project]"
- Offer value to gatekeeper: "I have information that will save [name] time/money"
- Be respectful but persistent — gatekeepers respect confidence
- Use "urgent callback" sparingly — overused tactic that backfires

**The Bridge Technique:**
"[Gatekeeper name], I understand you're protecting [CEO name]'s time. I work with [similar companies] on [specific problem]. Would it be worth [CEO] 5 minutes to see if this applies to [Company]?"

**Identifying the Real Gatekeeper:**
- Executive assistants often have more influence than you think
- Ask: "Who else is involved in decisions about [topic]?" to map stakeholders
- LinkedIn: search for "Executive Assistant to [CEO name]"

### SKILL 16: DEMO BOOKING

**Definition:** The skill of converting an initial interest or qualification conversation into a committed meeting or demo.

**Key Booking Tactics:**
- **Calendar link:** Always offer your Calendly / HubSpot link — reduces friction
- **Specific times:** "Does Tuesday at 2pm or Wednesday at 10am work better?" (2-option close)
- **Time commitment framing:** "It's just 20 minutes — I'll show you the key things, you tell me if it makes sense"
- **Value preview:** "In 20 minutes I'll show you exactly how [similar company] achieved [outcome]. Worth blocking the time?"
- **Avoid vague:** Never say "when you have time" — always offer specific options
- **Confirm twice:** Confirm via email AND LinkedIn/text for lower no-show rates
- **Reminder sequence:** Send 24-hour + 1-hour reminders with agenda

**Demo Booking Objections + Responses:**
- "Send me a video instead": "Absolutely — but just so you know, it changes based on your use case. A 20-minute call lets me show you what's most relevant for [their specific situation]."
- "I need to check with my team": "Great — can I send you a calendar invite for [day/time] to walk through it together? That way they can add questions too."
- "Can we do a quick call instead of a full demo?": "Of course — how about 15 minutes on [day]? I'll structure it around your specific questions."

### SKILL 17: PIPELINE MANAGEMENT

**Definition:** Actively managing your sales pipeline to ensure consistent deal flow, accurate forecasting, and efficient use of time.

**Pipeline Stages:**
1. **Lead:** Raw prospect data, unqualified
2. **Contacted:** Initial outreach sent, no reply yet
3. **Qualified:** Confirmed need, authority, urgency, budget
4. **Meeting Booked:** Demo/meeting scheduled
5. **Demo Given:** Presented solution
6. **Proposal:** Proposal or quote sent
7. **Negotiation:** Active negotiation
8. **Closed Won/Lost:** Final state

**Pipeline Management Rules:**
- Maintain minimum 3x pipeline coverage (if quota is $100K, have $300K in pipeline)
- Review pipeline weekly: stalled deals, next steps due, at-risk deals
- Use "3 Ds" for deal review: Dangling (stalled), Developing (moving), Done (won/lost)
- Identify risk early: no contact in 7+ days = risk signal
- Always know the next step and next step date for every open deal
- Keep deals moving: momentum is everything in sales

### SKILL 18: RAPPORT BUILDING

**Definition:** Creating genuine human connection with prospects to build trust, differentiate from competitors, and make the buying journey enjoyable.

**Rapport Tactics:**
- **Find common ground:** Shared alma mater, former employer, hometown, industry background
- **Active listening:** Use their own words back to them ("So it sounds like the main issue is...")
- **Genuine curiosity:** Ask about their role, career journey, what they're building
- **Humor (appropriate):** Lighten the conversation when appropriate
- **Compliment sincerely:** Their LinkedIn, their company's move, their team's work
- **Mirror their style:** Match their communication pace and formality

**Rapport Questions:**
- "What are you working on that you're most excited about right now?"
- "How did you get into [their industry/role]?"
- "What does a typical day look like for you?"
- "What made [Company]'s [recent news item] happen?"
- "I noticed you [spoke at event / published post] — what did you take away from that?"

**Avoid:**
- Being overly familiar with people you've just met
- Making assumptions about their personal life
- Interrupting or talking over them
- Being robotic or script-y

### SKILL 19: VALUE PROPOSITION FRAMING

**Definition:** Clearly and compellingly articulating the unique value your solution delivers — in terms the buyer cares about (outcomes, ROI, risk reduction).

**Value Prop Components:**
1. **What you do:** Your category + approach
2. **Who you do it for:** Specific ICP (role + company type)
3. **What outcome you deliver:** Quantified if possible
4. **Why you're different:** Your unique mechanism/approach

**Frameworks:**

**FEATURE → BENEFIT → VALUE:**
- Feature: "We use AI to score leads in real time"
- Benefit: "You know instantly which leads to prioritize"
- Value: "Your reps spend time on the right leads, closing more deals per day"

**The One-Sentence Value Prop:**
"We help [ICP] to [achieve measurable outcome] by [differentiation], so you can [end-state]."

Example: "We help B2B SaaS SDRs to book 3x more meetings by using AI-powered personalization at scale, so your team can hit quota without burning out."

**Value Props Should Be:**
- Specific, not generic ("reduce costs" → "reduce CAC by 30%")
- Outcome-oriented, not feature-oriented
- Framed around the buyer, not you
- Differentiated from competitors

### SKILL 20: COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE

**Definition:** Gathering and using information about competitors to position your solution effectively, handle competitive deals, and win more often.

**Competitive Intelligence Gathering:**
- Monitor G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, Gartner for competitor reviews
- Track LinkedIn for competitor product launches, new hires, funding news
- Use Crunchbase, PitchBook for competitor funding and valuation data
- Follow competitor sales teams on LinkedIn for messaging insights
- Attend same conferences/events competitors attend

**Competitive Positioning Tactics:**
- **Know your win/loss rate vs. each competitor**
- **Acknowledge competitor presence:** "You may have seen [Competitor]..." then reframe
- **Use competitor reviews:** "I saw a review that said [specific pain point] — we've solved that"
- **Lead with your differentiation:** Don't play defense, play offense on your strengths
- **Competitive battle cards:** One-pagers on each major competitor (win/loss rate, objections raised, positioning angles)

**Common Competitive Situations:**
- "You're more expensive": "We're X% more, but clients using us see Y% better [outcome]. The math works."
- "Competitor has more features": "Features don't matter if they're not being used. We've focused on the 20% that delivers 80% of results."
- "Competitor is already integrated with our stack": "We integrate with [their key tools] and go deeper in [specific area]."

### SKILL 21: TIME MANAGEMENT & ACTIVITY METRICS

**Definition:** Managing time effectively to maximize output across prospecting, outreach, follow-up, admin, and continuous improvement.

**Activity Metrics (Daily/Weekly Targets):**

| Activity | Daily Target | Weekly Target |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Cold Calls | 50-100 | 250-500 |
| Emails Sent | 100-200 | 500-1000 |
| LinkedIn DMs | 20-50 | 100-250 |
| Meetings Booked | 2-4 | 10-20 |
| New Leads Added | 30-50 | 150-250 |

**Time Blocking for SDRs:**
- **Block 1 (Morning):** High-energy prospecting — calls, LinkedIn outreach
- **Block 2 (Mid-day):** Meetings, demos, follow-up calls
- **Block 3 (Afternoon):** Admin (CRM logging), email follow-up, strategy
- **Block 4 (End of day):** Review pipeline, plan next day

**SDR Time Allocation:**
- 35-40% Prospecting (calls, research, building lists)
- 25-30% Outreach (emails, DMs, sequences)
- 15-20% Meetings/Demos (booked meetings)
- 10-15% Admin (CRM, reporting, planning)
- 5% Learning/improvement (call review, training)

**Best Practices:**
- Batch similar activities (all calls together, not scattered)
- Use the "2-minute rule": If it takes <2 min, do it now
- Protect deep work blocks from meetings/interruptions
- Track time in 15-min blocks to find efficiency leaks
- Review metrics weekly — adjust tactics based on what works

### SKILL 22: MENTAL RESILIENCE & REJECTION HANDLING

**Definition:** Building the psychological stamina required to sustain high-volume outbound activity, handle rejection gracefully, and maintain peak performance over time.

**The Psychology of Outbound:**
- Rejection rate in SDR work: 80-95% (most people don't respond/convert)
- Top SDRs normalize rejection — it's data, not personal
- Emotional resilience is a skill that improves with practice and intentional framing

**Resilience Tactics:**
- **Reframe rejection:** "Not interested right now" ≠ "Never." 40% of cold leads convert on 4th-8th touch.
- **Celebrate small wins:** Reply rate going from 5% to 8% is huge — acknowledge it
- **Process vs. outcome:** Focus on activity quality, not just results (you control inputs, not outputs)
- **Daily reset:** Rejection on one call doesn't affect the next — treat each interaction fresh
- **Peer support:** Share wins and losses with teammates — isolation makes rejection feel worse
- **Call review:** Listen to your own calls — most reps are surprised at how they sound
- **Physical stamina:** Sleep, exercise, diet affect resilience — take care of the body

**The "Same Day Reset" Technique:**
- After a string of rejections, take 2 minutes: stand up, breathe, reset posture
- Reframe: "Every call is one closer to the yes."
- Resume with renewed energy — recipients can hear your energy on calls

**Long-Term Sustainability:**
- Pace yourself — sprints vs. marathons
- Take PTO — real recovery prevents burnout
- Find meaning: connect daily outreach to the real outcomes your product delivers for customers
- Track your "why" — your personal mission statement

## Outbound Sequence Templates

### Channel: Email + LinkedIn + Cold Call (5-Touch)

#### Touch 1 — LinkedIn Connection Request (Day 0)
```
Hi [First Name],

Noticed you recently [posted about / spoke at / led] [specific topic]. 
[The insight about X] was particularly interesting — it's something 
we hear about a lot from [their industry] leaders.

Would love to connect and compare notes.

[Your Name]
```

#### Touch 2 — LinkedIn DM / InMail (Day 2)
```
Hi [First Name] —

Thanks for connecting. Quick question: are you currently working on 
[relevant challenge] at [Company]?

Most [their role] leaders I speak with are either tackling [challenge A] 
or struggling with [challenge B]. Curious where [Company] sits on this.

Happy to share what we're seeing peers do — no pitch, just a compare-notes conversation.

[Your Name]
```

#### Touch 3 — Cold Email (Day 4)
```
Subject: Quick question about [Company]'s [specific initiative/challenge]

Hi [First Name],

I spent a few minutes on [Company]'s [page/news/case study] and noticed 
[something specific]. [One-sentence observation or question about it].

That made me think about [their industry] — specifically, most teams we 
work with are trying to [achieve outcome]. The ones who do it best seem 
to [specific behavior or approach].

Curious — is that something you're thinking about at [Company]?

Happy to share what we're seeing others do if it's useful.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

#### Touch 4 — Cold Call + Voicemail (Day 6)
```
[PHONE — Ring once or twice, then leave VM]

"Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I'm reaching out 
because [personalized hook — their recent post, news, or industry challenge]. 
We help [their role] leaders at companies like [Company] to [outcome].

If that's relevant, I'd love to share what we're seeing. My direct line 
is [your number] — feel free to text or call anytime. Thanks!"
```

#### Touch 5 — LinkedIn Post Reply / Final Email (Day 9-10)
```
[Option A — Engage their LinkedIn post with a comment:]

"Just saw your post on [topic] — you're right that [specific point]. 
We've been working with teams on this exact challenge, and the ones who 
tackle it by [approach] tend to see [outcome].

Happy to share more if useful — here's a quick framework we use: [brief insight].

[Your Name]"

[Option B — Breakup Email if no response:]

"Hi [First Name], I tried to reach you a few times about [topic] — 
I'll assume this isn't the right timing.

Quick offer: if you ever want to compare notes on [their industry/challenge], 
I do this with [their peers] regularly. No obligation — just send me a note 
whenever you're ready.

Best,
[Your Name]"
```

### Quick Reference: SDR Command Bar

**Qualification Questions:**
- "What does your current [process/tool] look like?"
- "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?"
- "What would making this happen mean for you/your team?"
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "What's your timeline for solving this?"

**Objection Killers:**
- "That's a great point. What would you need to see to know this is worth the investment?"
- "Can I share what [similar company] achieved and let you decide if it applies?"
- "What would have to be true for this to be a priority?"
- "If budget weren't a constraint, would you move forward?"
- "What's standing between where you are today and where you want to be?"

**Meeting Booking Lines:**
- "Does Tuesday at 2pm or Wednesday at 10am work better for you?"
- "I'll send you a calendar invite — what's the best email?"
- "Just 20 minutes — I'll make it worth your time."
- "Can I send over a Calendly link so you can grab a slot that works?"
